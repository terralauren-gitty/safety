📺 Section Title Card
GENERAL SHOP RULES

The 8 Non-Negotiables

🎙 Narration

Before anything else: every person in this shop follows the same rules, every single time. These aren't suggestions — they're the foundation of everyone's safety.

📺 Rule Cards (sequential)
  • Rule 1 — Authorization First. Never operate a machine you haven't been trained and authorized to use.
  • Rule 2 — No Distractions. No phones, headphones, or horseplay when operating equipment.
  • Rule 3 — Dress for It. No loose clothing, dangling jewelry, or open-toed shoes. Tie back long hair.
  • Rule 4 — Stay Alert. Never operate machinery while tired, under medication that causes drowsiness, or impaired in any way.
  • Rule 5 — Lock It Out / Tag It Out. Before performing any maintenance or clearing jams, isolate all energy sources using LOTO procedures.
  • Rule 6 — Keep It Clean. Clean your workstation before and after use. Chips, oil, and clutter are hazards.
  • Rule 7 — Report Everything. Near misses, small injuries, equipment issues — report them immediately. No judgment, no exceptions.
  • Rule 8 — Know Your Exits. Be aware of all emergency exits, fire extinguisher locations, and first aid kits before you start work.
🎙 Narration

These eight rules apply to every person in the shop — from your first day to your thousandth. When in doubt, stop, assess, and ask a supervisor. There's no such thing as a stupid safety question.

📺 Section Title Card
PERSONAL PROTECTIVE
EQUIPMENT

Your Last Line of Defense

🎙 Narration

PPE doesn't prevent accidents — but it's often what stands between an incident and a serious injury. Think of it as your personal armor. It only works when you wear it — every time.

📺 PPE Checklist — Required at All Times
  • 👓 Safety Glasses or Goggles — ANSI Z87.1 rated. Required on the shop floor.
  • 🦺 Safety Footwear — Steel-toed boots or shoes with slip-resistant soles.
  • 🧤 Gloves — Cut-resistant for handling stock. Remove when operating rotating equipment.
  • 🎧 Hearing Protection — Required in designated high-noise zones (>85 dB).
  • 😷 Respiratory Protection — Required when machining materials that produce fine dust or fumes (e.g., aluminum, composites).
🎙 Narration

Note: gloves are critical for material handling, but should NEVER be worn while operating rotating equipment like lathes or drill presses. A spinning shaft can catch a glove and pull your hand in before you can react. Know when to wear them — and when to take them off.

📺 Warning Highlight
⚠ Critical

NEVER wear gloves while operating lathes, mills, or drill presses.
Rotating machinery can grab loose material instantly.

Script

📺 Section Header
EYE PROTECTION:
CHOOSE THE RIGHT TOOL
🎙 Narration

Eye injuries are among the most common — and most preventable — injuries in machine shops. Flying chips, sparks, coolant, and fine particulates can cause permanent damage in milliseconds.

📺 Protection Types
  • Safety Glasses — Everyday use on the shop floor. Must be ANSI Z87.1+ rated. Protects from impact, chips.
  • Splash Goggles — Required when working with coolants, cutting fluids, or chemicals. Sealed against the face.
  • Face Shield — Required for grinding operations. Worn OVER safety glasses, not instead of them.
📺 Inspect Before Use
  • Check for cracks, scratches, or distortion
  • Look for the ANSI Z87.1 marking on the lens or frame
  • Damaged eyewear must be replaced — never worn
  • Shared eyewear must be sanitized before use
🎙 Narration

If you're ever unsure which level of eye protection a task requires, err on the side of more protection — or ask your supervisor before starting. Your vision is irreplaceable.

📺 Section Title
KNOW YOUR HAZARDS

See it. Name it. Control it.

🎙 Narration

Hazard awareness is the ability to recognize risks before they become accidents. In a machine shop, hazards come in four main categories. Let's walk through each.

📺 Hazard Type 1 — Mechanical (Red)
🔴 Mechanical
  • Rotating parts: spindles, chucks, shafts, gears
  • Sharp cutting tools and edges
  • Hot metal chips (can exceed 500°C)
  • Flying debris from grinding operations
  • Pinch points on presses and clamps

Control: machine guards, proper work-holding, no bypass of safety interlocks

📺 Hazard Type 2 — Chemical (Orange)
🟠 Chemical / Fire
  • Cutting fluids and coolants — skin irritants, slipping hazard
  • Machining oils and lubricants
  • Metal dust from aluminum, magnesium — combustion risk
  • Solvent vapors from cleaning agents

Control: ventilation, proper storage, no open flames near machining fluids, SDS sheets available

📺 Hazard Type 3 — Electrical (Yellow)
🟡 Electrical
  • Damaged power cords or plugs
  • Water/coolant near electrical panels
  • Unauthorized electrical work
  • Overloaded circuits

Control: inspect cords before use, report damage immediately, LOTO before any electrical work

📺 Hazard Type 4 — Ergonomic (Blue)
🔵 Ergonomic
  • Repetitive motion: gripping, twisting, hand-arm vibration
  • Awkward postures during setup or inspection
  • Heavy lifting — stock material, fixtures, vises
  • Standing on hard concrete for long periods

Control: use lifting aids, rotate tasks, anti-fatigue mats, ask for help with loads >50 lbs

🎙 Narration

You'll learn the specific hazards of each machine during your machine-specific training. For now, practice the habit of "Stop — Look — Think" before starting any new task: identify potential hazards, confirm controls are in place, then proceed.

📺 Section Title Card (Red Background)
EMERGENCY
PROCEDURES

Know these before you need them.

📺 Emergency Stop (E-Stop)
⚠ Critical Knowledge
  • Every machine has a red E-stop button — know where it is before you start
  • Pressing E-stop immediately cuts power to the machine
  • Use it for: entanglement, uncontrolled operation, or any imminent danger
  • After use, do NOT restart machine — report to supervisor first
📺 Scenario: Injury or Medical Emergency
  • Step 1: Stop the machine immediately (E-stop or switch off)
  • Step 2: Call for help — shout, or use the emergency intercom
  • Step 3: Do NOT move an injured person unless they are in immediate danger
  • Step 4: Send someone to meet emergency services at the entrance
  • Step 5: Preserve the scene for incident investigation
📺 Scenario: Fire
  • Sound the fire alarm immediately
  • Evacuate — use nearest emergency exit, do not use elevators
  • Only use a fire extinguisher if the fire is small, you have an exit behind you, and you're trained to use it (PASS: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep)
  • Muster point: [identify on facility map]
  • Account for all personnel before giving all-clear
📺 Scenario: Chemical Spill
  • Alert coworkers and clear the area
  • Do NOT walk through the spill
  • Consult the Safety Data Sheet (SDS) — located at [location]
  • Report to supervisor and use designated spill kit
  • For skin/eye contact: flush with water for 15+ minutes at emergency eyewash station
🎙 Narration

Your supervisor will walk you through this facility's specific emergency contacts, muster points, and first aid locations during your orientation. Write them down. Store them in your phone. Know them before day one.
