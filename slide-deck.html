Section One
General
Shop Rules

The 8 Non-Negotiables

Apply to every person, every day
1

Authorization First

Never operate equipment you haven't been trained and authorized to use.

2

No Distractions

No phones, headphones, or horseplay when operating equipment.

3

Dress for It

No loose clothing, dangling jewelry, or open-toed shoes. Tie back long hair.

4

Stay Alert

Never operate machinery while tired, on drowsy medication, or impaired.

5

Lock Out / Tag Out

Before maintenance or clearing jams, isolate all energy sources (LOTO).

6

Keep It Clean

Clean your workstation before and after use. Chips, oil, clutter = hazards.

7

Report Everything

Near misses, small injuries, equipment issues — report immediately, no exceptions.

8

Know Your Exits

Know emergency exits, fire extinguisher locations, and first aid before you start.

Section Three
Hazard
Awareness

4 Hazard Categories

See it · Name it · Control it
🔴 Mechanical
Moving Parts & Edges
  • Rotating spindles, chucks, shafts
  • Sharp cutting tools & edges
  • Hot metal chips (>500°C)
  • Flying grinding debris
🟠 Chemical / Fire
Fluids & Combustion
  • Cutting fluids — skin & slip hazard
  • Metal dust — fire/explosion risk
  • Solvents and cleaning agents
  • SDS sheets must be accessible
🟡 Electrical
Power & Panels
  • Damaged cords or plugs
  • Coolant near electrical panels
  • Unauthorized electrical work
  • Always use LOTO procedures
🔵 Ergonomic
Body & Fatigue
  • Repetitive gripping & twisting
  • Awkward postures during setup
  • Heavy lifting — get help >50 lbs
  • Long standing — use anti-fatigue mats

Emergency

Emergency Response

Know these before day one
🔴
Machine Hazard
  • 1. Hit E-stop immediately
  • 2. Clear the area
  • 3. Notify supervisor
  • 4. Do not restart machine
🚨
Injury / Medical
  • 1. Stop machine, call for help
  • 2. Do not move injured person
  • 3. Send someone to meet EMS
  • 4. Preserve the scene
🔥
Fire
  • 1. Sound the fire alarm
  • 2. Evacuate via nearest exit
  • 3. PASS: Pull·Aim·Squeeze·Sweep
  • 4. Muster at designated point
